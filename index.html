Why I'm Building EPSIOTA — and Why Now

Ankit Kumar Rao · June 2026 · 6 min read

Most startups begin with an idea. Mine began with a problem I watched my family fight for years — invoices buried in WhatsApp chats, material costs estimated on gut feel, safety on construction sites managed by shouting across scaffolding. I grew up around Prithviraj Construction, my family's business. And for as long as I can remember, I thought: there has to be a better way.

EPSIOTA is my answer. Not just a company name — a commitment. A bet that artificial intelligence can fundamentally transform how construction works in India, starting from the ground up, starting from one family's experience, and eventually reaching every small contractor, every builder, every site manager who has been operating blind for too long.

"The best startups aren't born in boardrooms. They're born in the middle of problems that nobody else bothered to look at closely enough."

The Problem No One Talks About

The Indian construction sector is a ₹43 lakh crore industry. It employs hundreds of millions of people. It builds the roads, bridges, hospitals, and homes that the country runs on. And yet, a massive chunk of it — especially at the small-to-mid scale — still runs on Excel sheets that are never updated, phone calls that are never logged, and cost estimates that are, frankly, guesswork dressed up in confidence.

Labour safety? A helmet and a prayer. Material waste? Calculated after the fact, usually when it's too late. Client communication? Scattered across platforms, no single source of truth. I'm not being harsh — I'm being honest about what I've seen.

The Gap

Large construction firms have enterprise software. Small contractors have nothing. EPSIOTA is built for everyone in between — the builders who are too big to ignore tech, and too underserved to afford the enterprise alternative.

What EPSIOTA Will Do

At its core, EPSIOTA will bring computer vision, AI-driven cost estimation, and intelligent site monitoring to construction businesses that have never had access to these tools before. Think: a drone flying over a site, flagging safety violations before an inspector shows up. A model that looks at your project specs and gives you a cost range — not after weeks of back-and-forth, but in minutes. A client portal that keeps everyone informed without a single phone call.

This isn't science fiction. These technologies exist. The gap is in making them affordable, accessible, and genuinely useful for Indian contractors who don't have a 10-person tech team on retainer. That's the engineering challenge I want to solve. And I'm starting to believe it's one I can solve — because I'm simultaneously building the technical skills to make it happen.

Why Me? Why Now?

I won't pretend to have all the answers. I'm a first-year student, juggling a BS in Data Science from IIT Madras, a B.Tech in CSE with an AI/ML specialization, and a certification program from IIT Roorkee. I'm learning DSA, studying machine learning, and trying to understand — every single day — how the pieces fit together.

But here's what I know: the best time to build is when you're hungry enough to figure it out. I don't have the luxury of waiting until I'm "ready." No one does. EPSIOTA isn't a five-year plan sitting in a slide deck. It's a living thing I'm growing alongside my education — using Prithviraj Construction as my first real pilot, my first real test, my first real proof of concept.

"I'm not waiting for permission. I'm building the foundation now — one line of code, one dataset, one problem solved at a time."

What Comes Next

Right now, EPSIOTA is an idea backed by relentless research and a very personal mission. The next milestones are clear to me: formalise the company structure, build the first working prototype for construction cost estimation, test it on real projects, and document everything — the failures included. Startups that are honest about what doesn't work move faster than those that only celebrate what does.

I plan to write here regularly — about the technical challenges, the lessons from building a startup while being a full-time student, the moments where AI genuinely surprised me, and the moments where reality humbled me. If you're a builder, a developer, or just someone who's ever looked at a broken system and thought "I could fix this" — this space is for you too.

EPSIOTA is just getting started. And so am I.
